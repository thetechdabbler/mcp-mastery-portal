## The setup

Resources and prompts are where teams **accidentally** rebuild shadow APIs—keep them narrow.

## Picture this

<Diagram id="resource-discovery-seq" caption="Client lists resources, reads URI, may subscribe to changes." alt="Sequence diagram resource list read subscribe" />

## Mental model

**Resources** answer “what exists to read?” **Prompts** answer “what instruction recipe should we apply?”

## Walkthrough

- Use URI templates for parameterized reads.
- Treat prompt bodies like **versioned code**—review changes.

## Hall of Shame

<HallOfShame tag="design" title="Tool-shaped reads">

```txt
tool("get_doc", { id: z.string() }, ...)
```

**Fix:** prefer `docs://{id}` resource unless reads must be side-effecting (they shouldn’t).

</HallOfShame>

## Why this matters in production

Resources are easier to cache, scan, and ACL than tools—use that.

## Mini challenge

Pick one read tool and convert it to a resource—what auth checks move where?

## Reflection

Which prompts in your org should require human approval to **publish**, not just to run?

## You can now brag that…

You can explain why your docs are resources without sounding like you reinvented HTTP GET (even if you kinda did, but politely).
